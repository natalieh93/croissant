A croissant (UK: ; US: , ; French pronunciation: [kÊwa.sÉ‘Ìƒ] ()) is a buttery, flaky, viennoiserie pastry of Austrian origin, named for its historical crescent shape. Croissants and other viennoiserie are made of a layered yeast-leavened dough. The dough is layered with butter, rolled and folded several times in succession, then rolled into a thin sheet, in a technique called laminating. The process results in a layered, flaky texture, similar to a puff pastry. Croissants are a common part of a continental breakfast in many European countries.
